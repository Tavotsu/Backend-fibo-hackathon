Brand Guidelines - Marketing Photography

PRODUCT PRESENTATION:
- Keep product centered and clearly visible
- Maintain brand integrity (logo, colors, packaging)
- Ensure label text is readable
- Product should be hero of the shot

LIGHTING:
- Prefer soft, diffused lighting for premium look
- Studio softbox, golden hour, or rim lighting
- Avoid harsh shadows on product

CAMERA ANGLES:
- Eye-level: neutral, trustworthy
- Three-quarter: dynamic, engaging
- Top-down flat lay: editorial, modern
- Low angle: bold, impactful

BACKGROUND:
- Clean, minimal for e-commerce
- Subtle lifestyle context for aspirational
- Blurred backgrounds (shallow DOF) to isolate product


PHOTOGRAPHIC GLOSSARY (Use these to enhance prompts):
- Lighting: Rembrandt (dramatic), Butterfly (glamour), Split (contrasty), Volumetric/God Rays (atmospheric), Neon Noir (cyberpunk), Softbox (commercial clean).
- Camera Angles: Low Angle (heroic), High Angle (vulnerable/overview), Dutch Angle (dynamic/tension), Macro (details).
- Lenses: 35mm (context), 50mm (natural), 85mm (portrait/product flattering), 100mm Macro.
- Aperture: f/1.8 (bokeh/blur background), f/8 (sharp everywhere).
- Film Stocks: Kodak Portra 400 (warm/grain), Fujifilm Velvia (saturated).

CINEMATIC STYLES:
- "Cyberpunk": Neon, rain, reflections, high contrast, blue/purple.
- "Minimalist Luxury": Concrete, shadows, plants, neutral tones, hard light.
- "Ethereal Nature": Fog, soft light, moss, water, floating elements, pastel tones.
- "Industrial Chic": Metal, brick, raw textures, dramatic shadows.

INSTRUCTIONS FOR SCENE DESIGN:
- Always define the Light Source direction.
- Define the Surface material (wood, glass, water, marble).
- Add Atmospheric elements (fog, dust particles, lens flare).
- NEVER assume a plain white background unless asked.

COLOR SCHEME:
- Use brand accent colors
- Neutral backgrounds with brand pops
- Maintain brand color consistency
